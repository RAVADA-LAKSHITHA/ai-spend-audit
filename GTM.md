# GTM.md — Go-to-Market Strategy

## Exact Target User

Not "startups" — that's too vague.

The exact user is an **engineering manager or CTO at a seed-to-Series A startup (5–50 people)** who personally approves SaaS spending. They are technical enough to understand what Cursor and Claude are, but too busy to audit every tool subscription monthly. They notice the bill when it arrives, sigh, and approve it without questioning whether each tool is still needed or right-sized.

Secondary user: a **developer advocate or platform engineer** at a slightly larger company (50–200 people) who has been asked by their CTO to "look into whether we're overpaying for AI tools" — and needs something concrete to show in a meeting.

These are not the people using the tools every day. They are the people paying for the tools every month.

The moment that triggers them is usually a finance review, a fundraise preparation, or a moment where someone on the team mentions they have three subscriptions that all do the same thing.

## How to Get the First 100 Users in 30 Days with $0 Budget

This is the exact plan, not "post on social media":

**Week 1 — Warm network (Days 1–7)**
Post on personal Twitter/X and LinkedIn: "I built a free tool that audits your AI tool spend and tells you exactly where you're overspending. No login, takes 2 minutes. Would love feedback from anyone who pays for Cursor, Claude, or Copilot." Tag 3 specific people you know who use these tools. Goal: 20 audits.

**Week 2 — Hacker News (Days 8–10)**
Submit a Show HN post: "Show HN: SpendSmart AI – Free audit tool that tells you if you're overpaying for AI tools." The title matters. Do not submit on a Monday morning — submit Tuesday or Wednesday between 8–10am EST when HN traffic peaks. A good Show HN with a working product can drive 200–500 visits in 24 hours. Goal: 50 more audits.

**Week 3 — Indie Hackers and Reddit (Days 11–20)**
Post in r/SaaS, r/startups, r/ExperiencedDevs: "I audited our team's AI tool spend and found we were paying for 3 tools that overlapped. Built a free tool so others can do the same." Include a screenshot of a real audit result. Reddit responds to authentic stories, not product pitches. Goal: 30 more audits from organic posts.

**Week 4 — Direct outreach (Days 21–30)**
Find 20 founders on Indie Hackers who have posted about their tool stack or expenses. Send a one-line DM: "Hey, I built a free AI spend audit tool — I think it might surface some savings for your stack. No login needed, takes 2 min: [link]." At a 30% response rate, that's 6 more active users who will share if they find value. Goal: final push to 100.

## The Unfair Distribution Channel

Credex already has relationships with companies that buy discounted AI credits. Every one of those companies is a warm lead for this tool — they already care about AI spend. Credex can embed SpendSmart AI into their sales process: "Before we talk about credits, run this free audit to see exactly where you're overspending." This turns the tool into a qualification layer for Credex's existing pipeline. No other competitor can do this because they don't have Credex's supplier relationships.
